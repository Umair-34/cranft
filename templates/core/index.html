NFT

What is an NFT?

NFT stands for Non-Fungible Token, but what the f*ck does that mean ? Right, sorry. “Non-fungible” means that it’s unique and can’t be replaced with something else. Basically an NFT is pretty much like a physical object, you can transfer it and can’t be duplicated. Of course you can create the same object, painting or NFT using the same techniques but it will not be exactly the same. In that game, like in the physical art market, the first created is the original and so the valuable one. Blockchain technology permits everyone to verify when, by who, how an NFT was created and who owns it. Technically an NFT is a unique piece of information representing a digital good (like an image) stored forever in a blockchain.

NFT

Your art forever

We're gonna get a bit technical here and talk about Bitcoin related stuff, sorry. Once turned as an NFT your art will last forever, why ? Thanks to blockchain technology and IPFS. IPFS is a decentralized system to store files, once a file is uploaded it is replicated across many computers around the world. It ensures that the file will always be accessible for anyone who knows it’s identifier. Then we use the Polygon Network (a layer 2 solution for Ethereum) to create a unique token (an NFT) that contains the IPFS identifier of your art. That means, nobody will be able to alter your art ever.

Why it’s free ?

The Cranft.art project aims to help independent artists, like art students, to make money from their passion. Earning money with art was quite tricky back in the days but hopefully we are in 2021 and there are many technologies to help. These technologies are Internet, Blockchain, Cryptocurrencies and NFT. Combined together they give superpowers to artists, Cranft.art is here to make it happen. Creating an NFT costs money, there are many fees along the way, but don’t worry we take care of that. Once an NFT is sold, the money (in cryptocurrencies) from the sale is sent to our wallet, then we send it to yours. This is where we take our cut. The rate is 70% for you, 20%* to pay fees and maintain the platform and 10%* for the founders. *These values can slightly vary.
